Ollie Lloyd Explain how expressions in math work In mathematics, an expression is a combination of numbers, variables, and mathematical operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Expressions can be used to represent a wide range of mathematical problems, from simple arithmetic to complex algebraic equations. Here are a few examples of expressions in math: 2 + 3 - 1: This expression contains three numbers and two addition/subtraction operators. 4 × (5 - 2): This expression contains two numbers, two operators, and a set of parentheses to indicate that the subtraction operation should be done before the multiplication operation. x² + 3x - 4: This expression contains two variables (x and x²), two numbers, and two operators. This is an example of a polynomial expression. When evaluating an expression, we typically follow a set of rules to perform the mathematical operations in the correct order.
